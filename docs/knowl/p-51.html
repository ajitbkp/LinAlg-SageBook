We can use the theorem TheoremÂ 1.1.9 to find the inverse of a matrix using elementary row operations. If a matrix \(A\) is non singular and is row equivalent to the identity matrix \(I\) by a sequence of elementary row operations say \(E_1,\ldots, E_k\text{,}\) then we have, \(I= E_k\cdot E_{k-1}\cdots E_1A\text{.}\) This implies \(E_k\cdot E_{k-1}\cdots E_1 \times I =A^{-1}\text{.}\) In other words, the sequence of elementary row operations required to turn \(A\) into \(I\) also turns \(I\) into \(A^{-1}\) . Thus to find inverse of \(A\text{,}\) we adjoin \(I\) to \(A\) on the right to give the augmented matrix \([A~|~I]\text{,}\) and perform elementary row operations on \([A~|~I]\) that successively reduces \(A\) to the identity matrix and then extract \(A^{-1}\) from the right half of the transformed augmented matrix.
