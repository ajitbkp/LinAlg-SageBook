Item 4.

Suppse \(B\) is a matrix such that \(AB\) exist. Also assume that columns of \(B\) are \(b_1,\ldots, b_n\text{.}\) In particular, that is, \(B=\begin{bmatrix} b_1\amp b_2\amp\cdots \amp b_n\end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} AB = \begin{bmatrix} Ab_1\amp Ab_2\amp\cdots \amp Ab_n\end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}

Similarly, we have

\begin{equation*} AB = \begin{bmatrix} R_1B\\ R_2B\\\vdots \\ R_nB\end{bmatrix}. \end{equation*}
