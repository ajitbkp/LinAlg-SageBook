Let \(A_3\) be the matrix obtained by adding 2 times the 2nd row to the 3rd rows of \(A\) in place of the 3rd row. Then \(A_3=EA\text{,}\) where \(E=\begin{pmatrix}1 \amp 0 \amp 0\\0 \amp 1 \amp 0\\0 \amp 2 \amp 1\end{pmatrix}\text{.}\) What is \(E^{-1}\text{?}\) It is easy to see that \(E^{-1}=\begin{pmatrix}1 \amp 0 \amp 0\\0 \amp 1 \amp 0\\0 \amp -2 \amp 1\end{pmatrix}\) whihc is obtained by adding -2 times the 2nd row to the 3rd rows of \(A\text{.}\)
