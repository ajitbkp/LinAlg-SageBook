Let \(\alpha_1,\ldots,\alpha_{k+1}\) be scalars such that

\begin{equation} \alpha_1 v_1+\alpha_2v_2+\cdots+\alpha_kv_k+\alpha_{k+1}v_{k+1}=0\tag{2.4.1} \end{equation}

Since \(\beta\) is a basis of \(W\text{,}\) for each \(i=1,2,\ldots, k+1\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} v_i = \sum_{j=1}^k a_{ij}u_j \end{equation*}

Substituting this in Equation (2.4.1), we get

\begin{equation} \alpha_1 \left(\sum_{j=1}^k a_{1j}u_j\right)+\cdots+ \alpha_{k+1}\left(\sum_{j=1}^k a_{k+1j}u_j\right)=0\tag{2.4.2} \end{equation}

Collecting the coefficients of \(u_i's\) in the Equation (2.4.2), we get

\begin{equation} \left(\sum \alpha_ia_{i1}\right)u_1+\cdots + \left(\sum \alpha_ia_{ik+1}\right)u_{k+1}=0.\tag{2.4.3} \end{equation}

Since\(\beta\) is lineary independent, we have

\begin{align*} \alpha_1 a_{11}+\alpha_2a_{21}+\cdots+\alpha_{k+1}a_{k+11} =\amp 0 \\ \alpha_1 a_{12}+\alpha_2a_{22}+\cdots+\alpha_{k+1}a_{k+12} =\amp 0 \\ \vdots \amp \\ \alpha_1 a_{1k}+\alpha_2a_{2k}+\cdots+\alpha_{k+1}a_{k+1k} =\amp 0 \end{align*}
