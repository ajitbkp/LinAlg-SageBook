Example 1.1.8.

  1. Let \(A=[a_{ij}]\) be a rectangular matrix of order \(3\times 4\text{.}\) Let \(A_1\) be a matrix obtained by interchanging 2nd and 3rd rows of \(A\text{.}\) Then \(A_1=EA\) where \(E=\begin{pmatrix}1 \amp 0 \amp 0\\0 \amp 0 \amp 1\\0 \amp 1 \amp 0\end{pmatrix}\text{.}\)

    What is \(E^{-1}\text{?}\)

  2. Let \(A_2\) be the matrix obtained by multiplying 2nd row of \(A\) by a scalar \(\alpha\text{.}\) Then \(A_2=EA\text{,}\) where \(E=\begin{pmatrix}1\amp 0 \amp 0\\0 \amp \alpha \amp 0\\0 \amp 0 \amp 1\end{pmatrix}\text{.}\) What is \(E^{-1}\text{?}\)

  3. Let \(A_3\) be the matrix obtained by adding 2 times the 2nd row to the 3rd rows of \(A\) in place of the 3rd row. Then \(A_3=EA\text{,}\) where \(E=\begin{pmatrix}1 \amp 0 \amp 0\\0 \amp 1 \amp 0\\0 \amp 2 \amp 1\end{pmatrix}\text{.}\) What is \(E^{-1}\text{?}\) It is easy to see that \(E^{-1}=\begin{pmatrix}1 \amp 0 \amp 0\\0 \amp 1 \amp 0\\0 \amp -2 \amp 1\end{pmatrix}\) whihc is obtained by adding -2 times the 2nd row to the 3rd rows of \(A\text{.}\)
