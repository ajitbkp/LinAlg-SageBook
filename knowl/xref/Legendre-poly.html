Example 7.1.22. Legendre Polynomials.

Consider \(V ={\cal P}_3(\R)\) with inner product \(\inner{p}{q}:=\int_{-1}^1 p(x)q(x)\,dx\text{.}\) Use the standard basis \(\beta =\{v_1,v_2,v_3,v_4\} = \{1,x,x^2,x^3\}\) to find an orthogonal basis of \({\cal P}_3(\R)\text{.}\)
ðŸ”—
First of all notice that \(\beta\) is not an orthogonal basis. For \(\inner{v_1}{v_3}=\inner{1}{x^2} = \int_{-1}^1 x^2 dx = \frac23\text{,}\) \(\inner{v_2}{v_4}=\int_{-1}^1 x^4 dx = \frac25\text{.}\) Also note that \(\inner{v_1}{v_2}=\int_{-1}^1 xdx = 0\text{.}\) \(\inner{v_2}{v_3}=\int_{-1}^1 x^3dx = 0\text{.}\) \(\inner{v_1}{v_4}=\int_{-1}^1 x^3 dx = 0\text{.}\) \(\inner{v_3}{v_4}=\int_{-1}^1 x^5dx = 0\text{.}\)
ðŸ”—
Since \(v_1\) and \(v_2\) are already orthogonal, we can choose \(u_1=v_1=1\) and \(u_2 = v_2=x\) in the Gram-Schmidt process. Next
\begin{equation*} u_3 = v_3-\inner{v_3}{u_1}/\norm{u_1}^2u_1-\inner{v_3}{u_2}/\norm{u_2}^2u_2x^2\text{.} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
We have
\begin{equation*} \inner{v_3}{u_1}= \int_{-1}^1 x^2 dx = \frac23, \inner{u_1}{u_1}=\int_{-1}^1 1dx = 2\text{.} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Hence
\begin{equation*} u_3 = x^2 - \frac{\frac23}{2} \times 1 = x^2-\frac13\text{.} \end{equation*}
ðŸ”—
Next
\begin{align*} u_4 \amp = u_4-\inner{v_4}{u_1}/\norm{u_1}^1u_1-\inner{v_4}{u_2}/\norm{u_2}^1u_2-\inner{v_4}{u_3}/\norm{u_3}^1u_3\\ \amp =v_4-\inner{v_4}{u_2}/\norm{u_2}^1u_2-\inner{v_4}{u_3}/\norm{u_3}^1u_3\\ \amp = x^3-\frac35 x\text{.} \end{align*}
ðŸ”—
Hence an orthogonal basis is \(\beta'=\{1,x,x^2-\frac{1}{3}, x^3-\frac35 x \}\text{.}\) These are the first four Legendre polynomials.
ðŸ”—
After normalizing the vectors, we get an orthonormal basis
\begin{equation*} \beta''=\left\{\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}, \frac{\sqrt{6}}{2}x, \frac{3\sqrt{10}}{4}\left(x^2-\frac13\right), \frac{5\sqrt{14}}{4}\left(x^3-\frac35 x\right)\right\}\text{.} \end{equation*}
