Four well-mixed tanks \(T_1, T_2, T_3, T_4\) form a recirculation/mixing network used to produce a sauce. At steady state, the concentration \(c_i\) (in \(kg\cdot m^{-3}\)) of a soluble ingredient ( say salt or sugar) in tank \(T_i\) satisfies the steady-state mass balance: \(\left(\text{total outflow from } T_i \right) c_i -\sum_{j\neq i} \left(\text{ flow from to } \right) c_j =\text{net external feed of ingredient to }.\) The system of equations can be written in matrix form as

\begin{equation*} A \mathbf{c} = \mathbf{b}, \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} A = \begin{pmatrix} 20 \amp -5 \amp -3 \amp 0\\ -4 \amp 18 \amp -6 \amp -2\\ 0 \amp -3 \amp 15 \amp -4\\ -2 \amp 0 \amp -5 \amp 17 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \mathbf{b} = \begin{pmatrix} 14\\ 20\\ 9\\ 11 \end{pmatrix}, \quad \mathbf{c} = \begin{pmatrix} c_1\\ c_2\\ c_3\\ c_4 \end{pmatrix}. \end{equation*}

For example, the first equation reads: \(20c_1 - 5c_2 - 3c_3 = 14\text{,}\) meaning the total outflow coefficient from \(T_1\) is 20, the inflows from \(T_2\) is 2 and \(T_3\) are 5 and 3 respectively, and the net external ingredient feed to \(T_1\) is 14 (in consistent units). Solution.
