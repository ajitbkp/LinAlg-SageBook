Definition 4.1.1. Abstract Vector Space.

Let \(V\) be a nonempty set with two operations \(+\colon V\times V\to V\) defined by \(+(x,y):=x+y\) and multiplication \(\cdot \colon \R\times V\to V\) defined by \(\cdot(\alpha,x):=\alpha \cdot x\text{.}\) Satisfying the following properties:

  1. A1: for all \(x,y\in V\text{,}\) \(x+y=y+x\text{.}\)

  2. A2: for all \(x,y,z\in V\text{,}\) \(x+(y+z)=(x+y)+z\text{.}\)

  3. A3: There exists \(\overline{0}\in V\) such that for all \(x\in V\text{,}\) \(\overline{0}=x+\overline{0}\text{.}\) This \(\overline{0}\) is called an additive identity.

  4. A4: for each \(x\in V\text{,}\) there is a vector \(x'\in V\text{,}\) such that \(x+x'=x'+x=\overline{0}\text{.}\) This \(x'\) is called an additive inverse of \(x\text{.}\)

  5. S1: for all \(\alpha\in \R\) and \(x,y \in V\text{,}\) \(\alpha(x+y)=\alpha x+\alpha y\text{.}\)

  6. S2: for all \(\alpha,\beta \in \R\) and \(x \in V\text{,}\) \((\alpha+\beta) x=\alpha x+\beta y\text{.}\)

  7. S3: for all \(\alpha,\beta \in \R\) and \(x \in V\text{,}\) \((\alpha\beta) x=\alpha (\beta x)y=\beta(\alpha x)\text{.}\)

  8. S4: for all \(x\in V\text{,}\) \(1\cdot x=x\text{.}\)

The set \(V\) with \('+'\) and \('\cdot'\) is called a vector space over \(\R\text{.}\) Elements of \(V\) are called vectors.
