Similarly

\begin{equation*} x = \begin{bmatrix}v_1\amp \cdots \amp v_n\end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix}d_1\\\vdots \\d_n\end{bmatrix}=Bx_\gamma. \end{equation*}

Thus we have

\begin{equation*} Ax_\beta=Bx_\gamma\implies x_\beta = A^{-1}B x_\gamma \text{ and } x_\gamma = B^{-1}Ax_\beta. \end{equation*}

The matrices \(A^{-1}B\) and \(B^{-1}A\) are called transition matrices. We denotes \(A^{-1}B\) by \([I]_\gamma^\beta\) and \(B^{-1}A\) by \([I]_\beta^\gamma\text{.}\) Note that \([I]_\beta^\gamma=\left([I]_\gamma^\beta\right)^{-1}.\)
