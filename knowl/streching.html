Example 3.4.19. Streching.

Let \(a\) and \(b\) be two positive real numbers and \(A=\begin{bmatrix}a \amp 0 \\0 \amp b \end{bmatrix}\text{.}\) Define \(T\colon\R^2\to \R^2\) by \(T(x)=Ax\text{.}\) Then (i) \(T\) is stretching by a factor \(a\) along \(x\)-axis and by a factor \(b\) along \(y\)-axis if \(a,b>1\) and (ii) \(T\) is contraction by a factor \(a\) along \(x\)-axis followed by a factor \(b\) along \(y\)-axis if \(0\lt a,b\lt 1\text{.}\) Refer to FigureÂ 3.4.20 for stretching along \(x\) by 2 and \(y\)-axes by 1.5.
