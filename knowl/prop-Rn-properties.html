Proposition 2.1.1. Properties addition and multiplication in \(\R^n\).

We list the properties of vector addition and scalar multiplication in \(\R^n\) which can be easily proved. I encourage the readers to prove them.

(i) for all \(x,y\in \R^n\text{,}\) \(x+y=y+x\text{.}\) (Commutative property)

(ii) for all \(x,y,z\in \R^n\text{,}\) \(x+(y+z)=(x+y)+z\text{.}\) (Associative property)

(iii) The zero vector \(\overline{0}=(0,0,\ldots,0)\) has the property, for all \(x\in \R^n\text{,}\) \(\overline{0}=x+\overline{0}\text{.}\) This zero vector, we shall denote by 0 and is called the additive identity. One can show that zero vector is unique.

(iv) for each \(x\in \R^n\text{,}\) there is a vector \(x'\in \R^n\text{,}\) such that \(x+x'=x'+x=\overline{0}\text{.}\) This \(x'\) is called the additive inverse of \(x\text{.}\) It is easy to see that \(x'=-x\text{.}\)

(v) for all \(\alpha\in \R\) and \(x,y \in \R^n\text{,}\) \(\alpha(x+y)=\alpha x+\alpha y\text{.}\)

(vi) for all \(\alpha,\beta \in \R\) and \(x \in \R^n\text{,}\) \((\alpha+\beta) x=\alpha x+\beta y\text{.}\)

(vii) for all \(\alpha,\beta \in \R\) and \(x \in \R^n\text{,}\) \((\alpha\beta) x=\alpha (\beta x)y=\beta(\alpha x)\text{.}\)

(viii) for all \(x\in \R^n\text{,}\) \(1\cdot x=x\text{.}\)
